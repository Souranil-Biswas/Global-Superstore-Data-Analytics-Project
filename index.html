Project Overview

This project involves comprehensive analysis of the Global Superstore sales and profit data from 2011 to 2014. It includes identifying trends in product performance, segment profitability, shipping efficiency, and regional contributions. The insights aim to guide strategic decisions in marketing, operations, and supply chain optimization.

Key Metrics

  • Total Sales: $10.5M
  • Total Profit: $1.23M
  • Units Sold: 173,000+
  • Time Period: 2011â€“2014

Insights & Analysis

  • Top Categories by Sales: Technology ($3.75M), Furniture ($3.41M), Office Supplies ($3.34M)
  • Top Segments: Consumer ($5.4M), Corporate ($3.19M)
  • Top Sub-Categories by Profit: Copiers, Phones, Chairs
  • Worst Sub-Categories by Profit: Tables, Supplies, Bookcases
  • Sales by Ship Mode: Standard Class is most used and most profitable
  • Order Priority: Medium priority orders generate most revenue and profit
  • Country Performance: US, Canada, Australia lead in profit. Bangladesh, Angola lag behind.

Profit Margin Evaluation

  • Technology yields the highest profit margins.
  • Office Supplies have healthy margins and consistent sales.
  • Furniture suffers from lower profitability; pricing optimization is needed.

Recommendations

  • Expand offerings in Technology and Copiers sub-category.
  • Phase out or bundle low-performing items like Fasteners and Envelopes.
  • Promote Standard Class shipping to maximize profit margins.
  • Use data-driven discounts, especially in Furniture category.
  • Focus marketing efforts on the Consumer segment and key regions like US and Oceania.

Tools & Technologies Used

  • Python: Used for data cleaning, outlier handling, and preprocessing.
  • Pandas: For data manipulation and analysis workflows.
  • Power BI: To create interactive dashboards and visual insights.
  • Excel: Initial data exploration and manual verification.
